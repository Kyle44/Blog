My Top 20 Favorite Games 2017 edition

I grew up with the Playstation 1 and 2, then later on my parents helped me and my brothers buy an XBox 360. Those were my 3 biggest consoles growing up, but we've had many more over the years.

In my list, I have a lot of older games that many of you may have never played. Many of these games are here because of nostalgic reasons. Let's get right into it!



20. WWE Smackdown Vs. Raw 2008

My favorite wrestling game to date. I love the gameplay, but what pushes it over the edge for me is the big wrestling names you can be and the General Manager mode. I love that you can be The Undertaker, Jeff Hardy, Edge, Randy Orton, Bret Hart, The Rock and so many other incredible wrestlers! One of my favorites is Batista for his running STO, running spear, and of course his devastating Batista Bomb. For the longest time, one of my favorite game modes in any game had been SvR 2008's General Manager mode. You can go solo or you can compete against your friends locally, up to 3 general managers (SmackDown, Raw, and ECW). The most fun part of the mode happens immediately as you start when you draft your brand's wrestlers. The rest of the fun is creating the rivalries and all of the epic matches for your wrestlers to compete in! I've played a lot of wrestling games, but this one takes the cake as my favorite of all time. Honorable mention goes to SvR 2009, for the ability to skip through songs on the menu. Why don't more games have that?

3. The Simpsons: Hit & Run

I've played through this game many times, and level 1 many more. This is unquestionably the best Simpsons game ever created. The gameplay and the voice acting makes this game's replayability a 10/10, not to mention the incredible level design. The game only contains 3 maps to play on, but you get to play the best map in the game for 3 different levels. The story isn't too bad either, but you do need to get through the first 2 levels for it to really start. Thinking about it, the story progresses very slowly, but the humor in the game makes up for it.
